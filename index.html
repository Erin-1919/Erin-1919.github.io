Hello world, I am Erin! I was born and raised in southeast China and achieved a BSc in GIScience at Nanjing Forestry University, China. Moving to Fredericton, Canada in 2016, I got to work on spatial analysis applied to the field of forest ecology at University of New Brunswick, where I received my MSc in Forestry. In 2019, I moved to Calgary, Canada and started my PhD journey in Department of Geomatics Engineering at University of Calgary. Now I am a PhD candidate, committed to analyzing and predicting natural disturbances and hazards by adopting Discrete Global Grids technologies. I call myself a GISer, with an ISTJ-A personality.
